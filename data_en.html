Attclimate dataset

The ATTCLIMATE Survey is a four-wave panel study of the Spanish population fielded between 2023 and 2025. It tracks citizen attitudes towards climate change and support for a broad range of mitigation policies over time, enabling the analysis of opinion dynamics in response to real-world climate events and political developments.

The ATTClimate project is publicly available through the CORA (Consorci de Serveis Universitaris de Catalunya) research data repository. The data are deposited at doi.org/10.34810/DATA3567.

Coverage and sample:

Wave Fieldwork period N
Wave 1 February 2023 4,764
Wave 2 April 2024 3,019
Wave 3 October–November 2024 3,644
Wave 4 2025 3,997

Thematic coverage includes:

  • Support for climate mitigation policies (carbon taxes, low-emission zones, renewable energy, etc.)
  • Beliefs about the origin and severity of climate change
  • Political ideology and party preferences
  • Institutional trust
  • Territorial identity
  • Sociodemographic characteristics

Available materials:

  • Harmonised dataset (all four waves stacked, in .rds, .csv, .sav and .dta formats)
  • Individual wave datasets (same four formats)
  • Codebook (variable descriptions, value labels, availability by wave)
  • Original questionnaires (Spanish and English)
